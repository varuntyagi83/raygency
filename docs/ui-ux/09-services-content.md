# Raygency Services Content

## Naming Philosophy

Clients don't care about "n8n" or "RAG" — they care about outcomes. Service names should answer: "What will this do for my business?"

| Technical Term | Client-Friendly Name |
|----------------|---------------------|
| n8n workflows | **Smart Workflow Design** or **Connected Operations** |
| RAG / Vector DB | **Business Intelligence Hub** |
| Lead gen + email outreach | **Lead Capture & Nurture** |
| AI assessment | **AI Readiness Assessment** |
| Custom integrations | **System Integration** |
| Automation audit | **Operations Clarity Session** |

## Core Services (Homepage Display)

### Service 1: Lead Capture & Nurture

**What it is:** An automated system that identifies, captures, and follows up with potential clients without manual effort.

**Tagline:** Never lose a lead to follow-up fatigue again.

**What's included:**
- Signal detection (LinkedIn activity, website visits, form submissions)
- Automated lead qualification
- Personalized outreach sequences
- CRM integration and tracking
- Handoff alerts when leads are ready to talk

**Who it's for:** Consultants, coaches, and service providers who want a steady flow of qualified leads without spending hours on manual outreach.

**Outcome:** "I went from losing leads in my inbox to having a system that nurtures them until they're ready to book."

---

### Service 2: Business Intelligence Hub

**What it is:** A searchable knowledge system that makes your business information instantly accessible—like having a research assistant who knows everything about your work.

**Tagline:** Your business knowledge, instantly accessible.

**What's included:**
- Document ingestion (PDFs, notes, transcripts, emails)
- Intelligent search and retrieval
- AI-powered answers to business questions
- Integration with existing tools
- Continuous learning as you add more content

**Who it's for:** Consultants and coaches with years of accumulated knowledge trapped in documents, notes, and past conversations.

**Outcome:** "I can now answer client questions in seconds by querying my own knowledge base instead of digging through folders."

---

### Service 3: Client Operations System

**What it is:** An end-to-end automation for client management—from onboarding to delivery to follow-up.

**Tagline:** Delight clients without the manual overhead.

**What's included:**
- Automated onboarding sequences
- Session scheduling and reminders
- Progress tracking and check-ins
- Document delivery and organization
- Offboarding and feedback collection

**Who it's for:** Service providers who want to deliver a premium client experience without hiring an operations assistant.

**Outcome:** "My clients feel taken care of at every step, and I barely lift a finger after the initial setup."

---

### Service 4: AI Readiness Assessment

**What it is:** A strategic session to evaluate your current operations and identify the highest-impact opportunities for automation.

**Tagline:** Know exactly where automation will create the biggest return.

**What's included:**
- 90-minute deep-dive session
- Current state workflow mapping
- Opportunity identification and prioritization
- Technology recommendations
- Implementation roadmap
- Written report with specific action steps

**Who it's for:** Business owners who know they need automation but aren't sure where to start or what's possible.

**Outcome:** "I finally have clarity on what to automate first and a roadmap I can actually follow."

---

### Service 5: Connected Operations

**What it is:** Custom workflow automation that connects your existing tools into a seamless system.

**Tagline:** Your tools, finally talking to each other.

**What's included:**
- Multi-tool integration (CRM, calendar, email, docs, etc.)
- Automated data flow between systems
- Error handling and notifications
- Documentation and training
- Ongoing support

**Who it's for:** Anyone tired of copying data between apps, missing updates, or doing manual tasks that should be automatic.

**Outcome:** "What used to take me 2 hours of weekly admin now happens automatically in the background."

## Service Page Structure (If Separate Pages)

Each service page should include:

1. **Hero**
   - Service name
   - One-line tagline
   - Brief description (2-3 sentences)
   - CTA button

2. **The Problem**
   - What frustration does this solve?
   - "Does this sound familiar?" scenarios

3. **The Solution**
   - What's included (bullet list)
   - How it works (brief process)

4. **Who It's For**
   - Specific client types
   - "This is for you if..." statements

5. **Results**
   - Outcomes clients can expect
   - Metrics if available

6. **CTA**
   - Book a Discovery Call
   - "Not sure if this is right for you? Let's talk."

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

❌ "n8n workflows" — Too technical
❌ "RAG implementation" — Jargon
❌ "API integrations" — Developer language
❌ "Agentic AI" — Buzzword
❌ "No-code automation" — Focuses on how, not what

✅ Focus on outcomes: "Never lose a lead again"
✅ Focus on feelings: "Finally have clarity"
✅ Focus on time: "Reclaim 10 hours per week"
✅ Focus on pain points: "Your tools, finally talking"
