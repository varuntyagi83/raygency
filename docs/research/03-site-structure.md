# Raygency Website Information Architecture

## Design Principles Applied
- **Homepage does 80% of the work** → Everything essential is above-the-scroll or in the first 3 sections
- **Reduce cognitive load** → Maximum 5 navigation items, single CTA per section, minimal text
- **Trust over tricks** → Specificity, methodology transparency, anti-position visibility

---

## 1. Sitemap

```
RAYGENCY
│
├── Homepage (primary conversion page)
│
├── How I Work (methodology + process)
│
├── Results (case studies/examples)
│
├── About (founder story + philosophy)
│
└── Contact (simple booking page)
```

**Why only 5 pages?**
- Your target audience (solopreneurs, small business owners) are busy and skeptical of consultants
- More pages = more cognitive load = more drop-off
- The homepage handles 80% of trust-building and conversion; other pages exist for those who want to go deeper

## 2. Homepage Section-by-Section Outline

### SECTION 1: Hero (Above the Fold)

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Tagline/Anchor** | Understand. Automate. Scale. |
| **Headline** | Stop trading hours for growth. Build systems that run without you. |
| **Subhead** | I help solopreneurs and small business owners replace manual, fragile processes with automation that saves time and grows revenue. |
| **Primary CTA** | Book a Discovery Call → |
| **Visual** | Clean, simple—NOT a stock photo of "tech." Consider: abstract flow diagram or calm visual representing "systems working in background" |

**Objections this section resolves:**
- "What do you actually do?" → Headline and subhead answer in plain language
- "Is this relevant to me?" → "Solopreneurs and small business owners" signals exact audience
- "Why should I care?" → "Stop trading hours for growth" speaks to their daily frustration
- "What do I do next?" → Single, clear CTA

---

### SECTION 2: Problem Acknowledgment (3 Cards)

**Section Header:** Sound familiar?

| Problem | One-liner |
|---------|-----------|
| **Fragmented Tools** | Your CRM, email, scheduling, and docs all exist—but don't talk to each other. You're the glue. |
| **Low-Leverage Work** | You're spending your best hours on follow-ups, admin, and tasks that don't need *you*. |
| **Knowledge Trapped** | Leads, client notes, and insights exist—but they're scattered, unsearchable, and forgotten. |

**Objections this section resolves:**
- "Does this person understand MY situation?" → Specific, relatable problems show deep understanding
- "Is this just another 'AI hype' consultant?" → No buzzwords; describes their actual daily pain
- "Am I the right fit for this?" → If they see themselves in these problems, they self-qualify

---

### SECTION 3: Outcome Promise (Proof Strip)

**Section Header:** What changes when systems work *for* you

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| 🕐 **Time reclaimed** | Clients typically recover 10-15 hours/week from automation |
| 💰 **Revenue enabled** | Systems that capture, nurture, and convert leads while you sleep |
| 🧠 **Cognitive load reduced** | Stop holding it all in your head; let the system remember |

**Design note:** This section should feel like a *pause*—visual breathing room. Use icons, not heavy text.

**Objections this section resolves:**
- "What will I actually get from this?" → Concrete outcomes, not vague promises
- "Is this worth my time/money?" → Quantified benefits create value perception
- "Can I trust these claims?" → Specific numbers (10-15 hours) feel credible; vague claims ("save tons of time") don't

---

### SECTION 4: How I Work (Methodology Preview)

**Section Header:** My approach

| Belief | What it means for you |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Leverage starts with understanding** | I don't lead with tools. I start by mapping how your business *actually* runs—not how it's supposed to. |
| **Systems should make things calmer** | Good automation reduces decisions and maintenance, not adds to them. |
| **Automation is a business decision** | Every system I build ties to a clear outcome: time saved, revenue enabled, or risk reduced. |

**Secondary CTA:** See my full process →

**Objections this section resolves:**
- "How do you work? Will you just push tools on me?" → Methodology transparency shows thoughtfulness
- "Will this make my life MORE complicated?" → "Calmer, not more complex" directly addresses hidden fear
- "Is this going to be a one-size-fits-all solution?" → "How your business actually runs" signals customization

---

### SECTION 5: Anti-Position Statement (Trust Differentiator)

**Design:** Simple callout box or highlighted quote

> "I don't sell one-off automations or tool setups without context. If a system doesn't meaningfully improve how your business operates, I won't build it."

**Objections this section resolves:**
- "Is this person going to upsell me stuff I don't need?" → You proactively address the fear
- "Can I trust this person to tell me the truth?" → Saying what you *won't* do builds integrity signal
- "How is this different from Fiverr automation gigs?" → Sets you apart from commodity providers

---

### SECTION 6: Social Proof (When Available)

**Option A (If you have testimonials):**

**Section Header:** What clients say

> "Quote from client about specific result" 
> — Name, Title, Company

**Option B (If testimonials are still in progress):**

**Section Header:** Built for businesses like yours

- Coaches scaling beyond 1:1
- Consultants tired of admin eating their day  
- Agencies wanting systems that don't break when they grow

**Objections this section resolves:**
- "Has this worked for people like me?" → Specific industries/types show relevance
- "Is this person experienced enough?" → Social proof reduces perceived risk
- "Can I trust the outcomes they're promising?" → Third-party validation is more credible than self-claims

---

### SECTION 7: Final CTA (Conversion Block)

**Section Header:** Ready to build systems that scale without you?

**Primary CTA:** Book Your Discovery Call →

**Supporting text:** In 30 minutes, we'll map your biggest operational bottleneck and explore whether automation can solve it. No pitch, no pressure.

**Objections this section resolves:**
- "What happens when I click?" → Describes exactly what the call includes
- "Is this going to be a high-pressure sales call?" → "No pitch, no pressure" removes fear
- "Why should I act now?" → Focus on "30 minutes" makes it feel low-commitment

## 3. CTA Strategy

### Primary CTA: "Book a Discovery Call"

**Where it appears:**
- Hero section (top of page)
- Final CTA section (bottom of page)
- Sticky header (optional, after scroll)

**Why this works:**
- Matches your conversion goal directly
- "Discovery" signals exploration, not commitment
- Specific action reduces decision friction

---

### Secondary CTA: "See my full process"

**Where it appears:**
- After "How I Work" section
- Links to: /how-i-work page

**Why this works:**
- Catches people who need more information before booking
- Keeps them moving deeper into the site instead of bouncing
- Doesn't compete with primary CTA (different placement, different intent level)

## 5. What NOT to Include (Based on Research)

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| ❌ Feature list (n8n, webhooks, Zapier logos) | Prospects care about outcomes, not tools |
| ❌ "AI" prominently in hero | Triggers skepticism; your positioning is about *systems*, not tech buzzwords |
| ❌ Multiple CTAs per section | Creates decision fatigue; one action per section |
| ❌ Team photos on homepage | You're positioning as a strategic partner, not "hire a person" |
| ❌ Awards or badges | Build trust through specificity, not external validation |
| ❌ Long paragraphs | Homepage is for scanning; details live on inner pages |
| ❌ Generic stock photos | Either use clean abstract visuals or nothing |

## 6. Page-by-Page Summary

| Page | Purpose | Key Elements |
|------|---------|--------------|
| **Homepage** | Build trust + convert | Hero, Problems, Outcomes, Methodology preview, Anti-position, CTA |
| **How I Work** | Deepen trust for skeptics | Full 3-belief methodology, process phases, what to expect |
| **Results** | Evidence for analytical buyers | 2-3 case studies: Context → Approach → Result |
| **About** | Humanize + philosophy | Your "why," background, what you believe |
| **Contact** | Remove friction | Calendar embed, simple form, no barriers |
